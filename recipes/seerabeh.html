Seerabeh

Description

Seerabeh is a tangy walnut and verjuice-based northern Persian sauce typically served with fish.
It can also be thickened slightly and used as a dip with crisp veggies and flatbreads.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup walnuts, finely ground to a paste
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed to a paste
  • 3/4 cup verjuice (plus more as needed)
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground tumeric
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Handful of fresh cilantro, tough stems trimmed, finely chopped
  • Fresh mint

Steps

  1. Place the walnut paste in a medium pan.
  2. Gently roast over medium heat, stirring often, until fragrant, about 5 minutes. Take care nit to burn it.
  3. Add the garlic, verjuice, salt, tumeric and pepper. Be careful: the verjuice will split as soon as it hits the hot pan.
  4. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer the sauce until it slightly thickens (it will thicken more as it sits), about 10 minutes.
  5. Transfer the sauce to a bowl.
  6. Stir in the cilantro and set aside to cool for a few minutes.
  7. Taste and add another splash of verjuice, and some salt and pepper if needed. It will most likely need it.
  8. Cover and let rest in the fridge for a day or two before serving.
